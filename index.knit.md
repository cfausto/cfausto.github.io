## <span style="color:  #63D4FD;"> Bio</span>
<br>
  Welcome to my page! I am a 1st year PhD Student at the Keck School of Medicine studying Development, Stem Cells, and Regenerative Medicine. I am highly interested in understanding the genetic regulatory networks and molecular signal pathways that drive developmental patterns, and hope to use these pathways to treat chronic disease and congenital abnormalities.

---
  
## <span style="color:  #63D4FD;"> Research</span>
  <br>
  I currently study human kidney development in the **<a href="https://lindstromlab.usc.edu/"><span style="color:  #63D4FD;"> Lindström Lab</span></a>**, with an emphasis on the nephron (filtration unit of the kidney). I am specifically interested in the Wnt/β-catenin pathway's link to the nascent nephron's cell identities and morphogenetic features. 

I hope to leverage *in vitro*  **<span style="color:  #63D4FD;"> hiPSC kidney organoids</span>**, **<span style="color:  #63D4FD;"> multi-omic sequencing</span>**, and **<span style="color:  #63D4FD;"> high-resolution imaging</span>** to create developmental blueprints for kidney organogenesis.

I am highly motivated to build off our past atlases of **<a href="https://sckidney.flatironinstitute.org/"><span style="color:  #63D4FD;"> human</span></a>** and **<a href="https://cello.shinyapps.io/kidneycellexplorer/"><span style="color:  #63D4FD;"> mouse</span></a>** nephrogenesis to create regenerative cell types for therapeutic discovery and renal replacement therapy.
